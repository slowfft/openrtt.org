# Real-time text systems, then and now

Real-time text systems date back to the early 1970s at the latest, possibly having been implemented in some capacity in the 1960s. Most real-time text systems have been discontinued or abandoned by their owners, leaving very few such systems alive today. Both ICQ and AIM, two extremely popular late messaging platforms, offered real-time text capabilities during the 1990s and late 2000s respectively but it never caught on.

### unix `talk`, `ntalk`, `ytalk`, `utalk`

- Created sometime between 1981 and 1983 for BSD.
- Early versions would interleave streamed text from participants on the same line; this was addressed later with each participant getting a separate section of the screen instead.
- [Wikipedia - Talk (software)](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Talk_(software)>)
- [elists.isoc.org/pipermail/internet-history/2002-December/thread.html#171](https://elists.isoc.org/pipermail/internet-history/2002-December/thread.html#171) [[WA](https://web.archive.org/web/20250114215852/https://elists.isoc.org/pipermail/internet-history/2002-December/thread.html#171)]
